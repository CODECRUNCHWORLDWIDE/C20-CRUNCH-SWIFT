# Week 16 — Challenges

The exercises drill basics. **Challenges stretch you.** This one takes 60–120 minutes and produces something you can commit to your portfolio and point at in an interview: proof that your app is fully operable by someone who cannot see the screen.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Operate the whole app with the screen curtain on](challenge-01-voiceover-only-operability.md)** — turn on VoiceOver, enable the screen curtain (so you genuinely cannot see), and complete every core task in Notes v1 — create, edit, tag, delete, filter. Find every spot where you get stuck or confused, fix each, and document the before/after with a screen recording of the app being fully operated blind. (~90 min)

Challenges are optional. If you skip them, you can still pass the week. If you do this one, you'll be measurably ahead — "here's a screen recording of me creating, tagging, and deleting a note with the screen black, and here are the five places I got stuck and the fixes" is the most convincing accessibility evidence there is, and exactly what a senior reviewer or an accessibility-conscious employer wants to see. The "operate it blind" instinct you build here is what separates an app that *passes the audit* from one that's genuinely *usable* — and it's the bar App Review and enterprise procurement actually hold you to.
