# Week 21 — Challenges

The exercises drill basics. **Challenges stretch you.** This one takes 90–150 minutes and produces something you can commit to your portfolio and point at in an interview: a Live Activity that the *backend starts, updates, and ends* over APNs, while the app is terminated — the full backend-driven lifecycle.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — Push-to-start a Live Activity from Vapor](challenge-01-push-to-start-from-vapor.md)** — register for the app-wide `pushToStartToken`, send a `start` push from the Vapor backend so an activity *begins* on a terminated app's Lock Screen, then send `update` and `end` pushes to drive its whole life over the wire. The most current-to-2026, most impressive surface in the track. (~120 min, device required)

Challenges are optional. If you skip them, you can still pass the week. If you do this one, you'll be measurably ahead — "I ship a Live Activity that my backend starts, updates, and ends over APNs while the app is closed" is the single most senior, most current iOS capability you can demonstrate in 2026. The backend-driven real-time discipline you build here is the capstone's Live-Activity rubric line, done to production depth, and it's exactly the system-design question ("design a Live Activity for a food-delivery order") in the interview-prep pack.
